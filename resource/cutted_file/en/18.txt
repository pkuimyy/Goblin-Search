AB This article endeavors to provide an overview on contemporary Chinese   philosophy. The focus is on contemporary Chinese philosophy in the   Chinese-speaking world, particularly after the 1950s, although   contemporary Chinese philosophy both in its inception in early 20th   century China and in the English-speaking world are also explored. In   addition to designating separate genres of contemporary Chinese   philosophical interpretation and construction, including some of the   major issues under discussion and debate as well as giving attention to   several representative scholars, this article also teases out the   historical contexts in which those issues emerged and developed, and it   highlights the salient feature of contemporary Chinese philosophy in   general.
